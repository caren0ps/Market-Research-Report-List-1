<p><p>The electric car market primarily consists of two types: Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) and Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs). PHEVs combine an internal combustion engine with an electric motor, allowing users to switch between electric power and gasoline, offering flexibility and extended range. In contrast, BEVs operate solely on electric power, relying on batteries for propulsion, resulting in zero tailpipe emissions. Both market segments cater to different consumer preferences, with PHEVs appealing to those valuing hybrid versatility and BEVs attracting eco-conscious buyers seeking fully electric solutions.</p></p>
